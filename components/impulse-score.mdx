The impulse score component displays a visual gauge that rates how impulsive a purchase is on a scale from 1 (genuine need) to 10 (pure impulse regret). It includes a color-coded progress bar, verdict headline, supporting reasons, and financial advice.

## When to use

Use this component when the user asks:
- "How impulsive is this?"
- "Rate this purchase"
- "Is this a bad idea?"
- "Score this"
- "How dumb is this?"

## Props

<ParamField path="productName" type="string" default="Item">
  The product being scored.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="score" type="number" default={5}>
  Impulse danger score from 1 (genuine need) to 10 (pure impulse regret). Be honest and harsh in your scoring:
  
  - **1-3**: Low risk - legitimate need or well-considered purchase
  - **4-6**: Moderate - want disguised as need, questionable timing
  - **7-8**: High risk - definitely impulsive, likely regrettable
  - **9-10**: Critical - pure impulse, almost guaranteed regret
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="verdict" type="string" default="Hmm...">
  A short, punchy verdict headline (e.g., "Your wallet is crying", "Absolutely terrible idea", "This can wait"). Keep it under 10 words.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="reasons" type="array" default={[]}>
  2-4 specific reasons supporting the score. Be concrete and personalized when possible:
  
  - "You already own 3 pairs of headphones"
  - "This product will be 30% cheaper in 2 months"
  - "You mentioned wanting to save for a vacation"
  - "You've been shopping when stressed lately"
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="advice" type="string" default="Sleep on it.">
  One sentence of financial wisdom or a roast. Can be helpful or sarcastic depending on the score.
</ParamField>

## Visual design

The component features:
- Dark themed card with golden "Impulse Score" badge
- Large score display (1-10) with color-coded risk level
- Animated progress bar that fills based on score
- Scale labels: "Need", "Want", "Impulse", "Regret"
- Verdict box with bullet-point reasons
- Italicized advice with left border accent

## Scoring guidelines

Be harsh and honest when scoring:

- **Score 8-10** if any of these apply:
  - User is shopping while emotional
  - Product is purely for status/flex
  - User has multiple similar items
  - Price is >50% of monthly income
  - "Limited time offer" or artificial urgency

- **Score 5-7** if:
  - Want but not need
  - Budget is tight
  - Alternative exists at lower price
  - Timing is questionable

- **Score 1-4** if:
  - Genuine necessity (broken phone, work equipment)
  - Well-researched and budgeted
  - Replaces worn-out item
  - Long-term value clear
